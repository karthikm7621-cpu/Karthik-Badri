COMPANY POLICY HANDBOOK

SECTION 1: LEAVE POLICY

1.1 Annual Leave
All full-time employees are entitled to 20 days of paid annual leave per calendar year. Part-time employees receive pro-rated leave based on their FTE. Annual leave must be approved by your line manager at least 3 working days in advance. Unused leave up to 5 days may be carried over to the next year with manager approval.

1.2 Sick Leave
Employees are entitled to 10 days of paid sick leave per year. For absences longer than 2 consecutive days, a medical certificate from a registered practitioner is required. Sick leave should be reported to your manager before 9:00 AM on the day of absence.

1.3 Maternity and Paternity Leave
Primary caregivers are entitled to 16 weeks of paid maternity leave. Secondary caregivers receive 2 weeks of paid paternity leave. Additional unpaid leave may be requested up to 12 months. Leave must be requested at least 4 weeks before the expected start date.

1.4 Bereavement Leave
Up to 5 days of paid bereavement leave is available for the death of an immediate family member (spouse, child, parent, sibling). Up to 2 days is available for extended family members.

SECTION 2: REMOTE WORK POLICY

2.1 Eligibility
All employees who have completed their probation period are eligible to request remote work arrangements. Remote work is subject to manager approval and role requirements.

2.2 Request Process
Submit a remote work request through the HR portal at least 5 working days before the intended start date. The request must include your proposed work schedule, home office setup details, and communication plan.

2.3 Expectations
Remote workers must be available during core business hours (10:00 AM to 4:00 PM local time). Regular check-ins with your team are mandatory. A dedicated, distraction-free workspace is required.

2.4 Equipment and Support
The company provides a laptop and necessary software. Internet connectivity is the employee's responsibility. IT support is available remotely during business hours.

SECTION 3: COMPENSATION AND BENEFITS

3.1 Salary Reviews
Annual salary reviews are conducted every January. Performance-based adjustments take effect from April 1st. Employees may request an off-cycle review after significant role changes.

3.2 Health Insurance
Comprehensive health insurance coverage begins on the first day of employment. Coverage includes medical, dental, and vision care. Dependents may be added during open enrollment or within 30 days of a qualifying life event.

3.3 Retirement Benefits
The company matches 401k contributions up to 5 percent of base salary. Vesting occurs over 4 years with 25 percent vesting per year. Financial planning resources are available through our HR portal.

3.4 Professional Development
An annual budget of 2000 dollars is available for training, conferences, and certifications. Requests must be pre-approved and relevant to your current role or career progression within the company.

SECTION 4: CODE OF CONDUCT

4.1 Workplace Behavior
All employees are expected to maintain a professional, respectful, and inclusive workplace. Harassment, discrimination, or retaliation of any kind will not be tolerated.

4.2 Confidentiality
Employees must protect confidential company information and client data. This obligation continues after employment ends. Violations may result in termination and legal action.

4.3 Conflict of Interest
Employees must disclose any outside activities that may conflict with company interests. Secondary employment requires written approval from HR.

4.4 Social Media
Employees represent the company online. Do not disclose confidential information or make statements that could damage the company's reputation. Personal opinions should be clearly identified as such.

SECTION 5: PERFORMANCE AND DEVELOPMENT

5.1 Performance Reviews
Formal performance reviews occur twice yearly. Goals are set at the beginning of each review cycle. Continuous feedback is encouraged through regular one-on-one meetings.

5.2 Promotion Process
Promotions are based on performance, impact, and role scope. Employees may self-nominate or be nominated by their manager. Promotion committees review cases quarterly.

5.3 Internal Transfers
Employees may apply for internal openings after 18 months in their current role. Manager notification is required before interviewing. Successful transfers maintain accrued benefits.

SECTION 6: EXPENSE REIMBURSEMENT

6.1 Eligible Expenses
Business-related travel, client entertainment, and approved training expenses are reimbursable. Personal expenses are not covered. Receipts are required for all expenses over 25 dollars.

6.2 Submission Process
Submit expense reports within 30 days of incurring the expense. Reports require manager approval. Reimbursement is processed within 2 weeks of approval.

6.3 Travel Policy
Economy class is the standard for air travel. Hotel accommodations must not exceed 200 dollars per night. Meal allowances follow government per diem rates.

SECTION 7: WORKPLACE SAFETY

7.1 Emergency Procedures
Familiarize yourself with evacuation routes and assembly points. Report safety hazards to facilities immediately. Active shooter and natural disaster drills are conducted quarterly.

7.2 Incident Reporting
All workplace injuries must be reported within 24 hours. An incident report form must be completed. Workers compensation claims are processed through HR.

7.3 Health and Wellness
The company provides ergonomic assessments upon request. Mental health resources include an Employee Assistance Program with confidential counseling services.

For questions about any policy, contact HR at hr at company dot com or extension 1000.
